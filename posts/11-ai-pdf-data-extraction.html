How to Extract Key Information from Long PDFs with AI

Published on July 8, 2026 • By Tweaknook Editorial

AI can help create a first-pass summary of a long PDF, but the result should be checked against the original document. This is especially important for contracts, policies, financial reports, research papers, and scanned files.

Step 1: Check the PDF

Open the document and try to select a sentence. If the text cannot be selected, the PDF may contain scanned images rather than searchable text. In that case, the tool needs optical character recognition, and names or numbers may be read incorrectly.

Also check whether the document contains confidential, personal, or regulated information. Do not upload sensitive material to a service unless its privacy and data-retention terms are appropriate for the document.

Step 2: Define the Extraction Fields

A specific request produces a more useful result than “summarize this PDF.” List the fields needed for the task, such as:

  • Document purpose
  • Effective date and deadline
  • Responsible person or organization
  • Required action
  • Costs, quantities, or thresholds
  • Exceptions and unresolved questions

Step 3: Use a Source-Grounded Prompt

Read only the attached PDF and prepare an extraction table.

Columns:
1. Topic
2. Extracted information
3. Page number or section
4. Supporting text
5. Confidence: High, Medium, or Low

Rules:
- Do not add facts that are not present in the PDF.
- If a field is missing, write "Not found in the document."
- Keep dates, names, quantities, and units exactly as written.
- If two sections conflict, show both and flag the conflict.
- Finish with a list of items that require manual review.

Step 4: Verify the Output

Start with the details that would cause the most harm if they were wrong. Open each cited page and confirm:

  1. The page actually contains the supporting text.
  2. The output did not remove a condition such as “unless,” “except,” or “subject to.”
  3. Dates, decimal points, percentages, and units match the PDF.
  4. Tables were read in the correct row and column order.

Common Failure Cases

  • Scanned pages: OCR may confuse similar characters such as 0 and O.
  • Complex tables: Multi-row headings and merged cells may be assigned to the wrong column.
  • Footnotes: Important exceptions may be separated from the main sentence.
  • Long documents: Some tools may not process every page in one request.
  • Ambiguous language: A summary can hide uncertainty that is clear in the original wording.

Important: For legal, financial, medical, or compliance documents, use the AI output only as a navigation aid. A qualified reviewer should confirm the final interpretation.

Useful Output Format

A table with evidence locations is usually more practical than a long narrative summary. It allows a reviewer to move directly to the relevant pages and correct mistakes without rereading the entire document.
