Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 4, 2026

At Tweaknook, accessible from https://tweaknook.com, the privacy of our visitors is one of our main priorities. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and how third-party services, including Google AdSense, may use cookies and similar technologies on our website.

Information We Collect

Tweaknook may collect limited non-personal information automatically when visitors access our website. This may include internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, Internet Service Provider (ISP), date and time stamps, referring and exit pages, device information, and general usage data.

This information is not used to personally identify visitors. It is used to analyze trends, administer the website, improve user experience, and understand how visitors interact with our content.

Log Files

Tweaknook follows a standard procedure of using log files. These files log visitors when they visit websites. The information collected by log files may include IP addresses, browser type, ISP, date and time stamps, referring and exit pages, and the number of clicks. The purpose of this information is to analyze trends, administer the site, track users' movement around the website, and gather demographic information.

Cookies and Web Beacons

Like many other websites, Tweaknook uses cookies to improve the browsing experience and understand how visitors use the website. Cookies are small text files stored on a visitor's device by the web browser. Cookies may be used to remember visitor preferences, analyze website traffic, and support advertising services.

Google AdSense and Advertising Cookies

Tweaknook may use Google AdSense to display advertisements. Third-party vendors, including Google, may use cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to this website or other websites.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables Google and its partners to serve ads to users based on their visit to Tweaknook and/or other websites on the Internet.

  • Users may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.
  • Users may also opt out of some third-party vendors' use of cookies for personalized advertising by visiting www.aboutads.info.

Third-party advertising partners may use cookies, JavaScript, web beacons, or similar technologies in their advertisements and links that appear on Tweaknook. These technologies are used to measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns and to personalize advertising content. Tweaknook has no access to or control over cookies that are used by third-party advertisers.

Tweaknook's Privacy Policy does not apply to other advertisers or websites. We encourage visitors to review the privacy policies of third-party advertising partners for more detailed information about their practices and instructions on how to opt out of certain options.

Visitors can choose to disable cookies through their individual browser settings. More detailed information about cookie management can be found on the websites of specific web browsers. Please note that disabling cookies may affect how some parts of the website function.

European, UK, Swiss, and US State Privacy Rights

Depending on a visitor's location, certain privacy laws may provide additional rights regarding personal data, cookies, advertising preferences, and consent choices.
